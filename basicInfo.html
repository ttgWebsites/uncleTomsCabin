Basic Info

  • Title
  • Uncle Tom's Cabin
  • Author
  • Harriet Beecher Stowe
  • Published
  • The National Era, 1851.
  • Genre
  • Social Protest, Anti- Slavery.
  • Setting
  • The novel is set sometime around the 1850's primarily in the southern states of the U.S. Kentucky and Louisiana primarily. It also takes place along the path to Canada from the south and also several Quaker Settlements.
  • Point Of View
  • The novel is told mostly in the third person, however sometimes in the second person.
  • Conflict
  • There is one primary conflict in the novel, slavery. The conflict encompasses both the physical and mental effects slavery puts upon the southern blacks.
