Interesting Details

  • The novel sold very well after its publication in the 1850's and many view it as an extremely important piece of literature in the abolitionist movement, which would eventually culminate in the Civil War.
  • A great controversy and uproar occurred in the southern states in response to the novel, as many slavery supporters viewed it as harmful to their beliefs. Stowe received hate mail, and one letter even included the ear of a slave.
  • Stowe remained committed to her cause even after publishing the novel, she would spend her life as a great advocate for human rights in general, not just black rights.
