Book Summary

  • So basically...
  • Arthur Shelby, a farmer from Kentucky, develops a large debt and must find a way to pay it off. In order to do so he plans to sell two of his slaves, Uncle Tom, and a young boy to a slave trader, Mr. Haley. The decision is tough on Arthur because he and his wife Emily had developed fond relationships with the slaves. Before the sale however Eliza, Emily's maid and the mother of the young slave who's being sold, over hears Emily and Arthur arguing about the sale. Out of fear for her child's well being, Eliza and her son escape after informing Uncle Tom of the sale. Eliza and her son flee Kentucky to the north where they plan to meet Eliza's husband George Harris and continue on to Canada. They seek refuge and help from a Quaker settlement. Their road is difficult as a group sent out by Haley to bring them backtracks them down. The group led by a man named Loker. George manages to shoot Loker preventing them from being captured. Yet due to Eliza's good heart they take Loker to a Quaker settlement where he is saved. Loker is changed by the incident and resolves to help Eliza, George, and their son escape to Canada. Meanwhile Uncle Tom reluctantly leaves with Haley and is placed on a boat that will take him to be sold. On the boat he befriends a young girl, Eva, who ends up falling in the river forcing Tom to jump in and save her. Tom's selfless act convinces Eva's father Augustine St. Clare to buy Tom. Tom's new owner is takes very good care of him and Tom shares his religious beliefs with Eva. Over the next two years Tom's relationship with the St. Clare family grows, and he becomes much closer with Eva. However Eva grows sick and eventually dies yet leaves a profound spiritual impact on all the people around her. Augustine agrees to free Tom, as feels no prejudice towards blacks and Tom has become part of the family. Shortly before being freed, Augustine is killed in a brawl, thus Tom isn't freed and he is sold to another master. Simon Legree, a plantation owner who is very cruel, becomes owner of Tom. Legree not only purchases Tom but a female slave Emmeline for sexual purposes. After refusing to follow some harsh orders from Legree, Tom is punished, and Legree develops a hatred of Tom. Tom develops a friendship with another slave Cassy during this time. He learns of her story and Legree's sexual abuse and how she lost a daughter to slavery. Tom suffers throughout but through his faith helps Emmeline and Cassy escape. Yet by doing so must suffer a fatal beating by the hands of his master. Tom dies a martyr shortly before he would have gained freedom, as his original master's son George had planned to purchase it. They had been close when Tom lived with the Shelby's. Emmeline and Cassy run into George Harris' sister on the boat to there freedom. There they discover that Eliza is Cassy's long lost daughter. The group travels to Canada and meets up with Eliza and George, where the family is reunited. The family travels to Liberia via Europe, where they establish a new life in a settlement for former American Slaves. George Shelby returns to his family farm in Kentucky after trying to save Tom. He was moved by Tom's selflessness and faith, and decides to free all the slaves on the farm while encouraging them to lead a faith led and selfless life much like Tom.
