Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • Uncle Tom's Cabin set in the 1800's begins on a Kentucky farm on an cold afternoon in February
  • Arthur Shelby, the owner of the farm is in debt, and has decided to sell two of his slaves to a slave trader, Mr. Haley.
  • Shelby purposes selling Uncle Tom, a hard working, religious, and honest man.
  • Uncle Tom is not enough so it is decided that a young boy, Harry, son of Eliza, a Maid
  • Before the sale is completed Mr. and Mrs. Shelby (Emily) must talk it over
  • Emily assures Eliza that her son Harry will not be sold
  • Chapter 2
  • This chapter gives background on Eliza and her husband George
  • Eliza who's very attractive, is married to George, a skilled mulatto
  • George and Eliza are separated by work, as he was sent to work in a factory
  • Rather ingeniously, George invented a machine that makes cleaning hemp quicker and easier
  • However the invention only earns George a spot working menial work, as his invention does not impress his master.
  • The couple having previously lost two children are very protective of Harry as he is there only living child
  • Chapter 3
  • Following Eliza and Mrs. Shelby's conversation regarding Harry, George comes to visit Eliza
  • He plans to escape to the north as he can't stand suffering anymore, and is being encouraged to marry another women by his master
  • Eliza demands he stay it out and put his faith in God but George is not deterred and plans to escape to Canada and work to purchase Eliza and Harry's freedom
  • Chapter 4
  • Back in Tom's cabin his wife Chloe (Aunt Chloe) is preparing dinner for Tom and there family
  • Joining the family is the Shelby's son, George or Mas'r George, who is working with educating Tom
  • George and Tom appear to be good friends as they talk and laugh throughout before eating
  • Following dinner Mas'r George reads from the book of Revelation as the slaves gather and sing hymns
  • Meanwhile Mr. Shelby works out the details of the sale agreeing to sell Tom and Harry and in turn be free of his mortgage payments
  • Shelby urges Haley that he must sell Tom to only a compassionate master, only to get the reply of 'I'll do my best'
  • Chapter 5
  • After the sale Mr. Shelby informs his wife of the sale
  • Disgusted, Emily argues with Arthur demanding he must cancel the sale and the act of slavery is sinful and she despises it.
  • Arthur tells his wife the sale is done and signed and that otherwise they would have to sell all of there property and that the proposition of her selling her watch instead of Harry could not be done
  • Eliza, listening to the conversation, learns of her son's fate. She immediately gathers her son, heads to Tom's cabin and informs him and Chloe, it is decided she will escape with Harry and meet up with George en route to Canada
  • Chapter 6
  • The following morning it is discovered that Eliza is missing, and assumed she has escaped
  • The escape is met with Joy from Mrs. Shelby however grief from her husband as he fears Mr. Haley will assume he aided the escape in order to save the boy
  • Haley shows up to collect Tom and Harry only to learn of the boys disappearance
  • Enraged he confronts Mr. Shelby and the two men discuss it, all the while Haley's antics begin to upset Shelby as he realizes he is not a good man
  • Shelby agrees to prepare a horse for Haley in order to pursue Eliza, and some of the slaves are sent to get the horse prepared
  • The slaves, wanting to help Eliza sneak a beechnut under the saddle in order to upset the horse when Haley attempts to ride
  • This causes some chaos, and ultimately prevents Haley from waiting till after lunchtime to begin pursuit
  • Chapter 7
  • The previous night when Eliza leaves Tom's cabin and begins the escape she is very worried and puts her faith in God's hands
  • She travel all night and eventually reaches the Ohio River, the border between north and south
  • Halfway frozen the river is impassable until later in the day, so Eliza and Harry are forced to take refuge in an inn
  • At the farm, lunch is prepared for Haley in a way to delay as much as possible, therefore not allowing Haley to begin search until about two in the afternoon
  • Escorted by Andy and Sam, two slaves, Haley is taken on a long route in search of Eliza yet eventually makes it to the river
  • Andy seeing Eliza through a window manages to alert her by letting out a yell
  • Eliza fearing for her son, grabs him and makes course across the icy river hopping from piece of ice to piece of ice, eventually making it across where she receives help and guidance from Mr. Symmes, who she knows as a farm owner
  • Chapter 8
  • Haley is completely unprepared and not capable of continuing the pursuit so he takes rest in a tavern
  • There he meets a slave hunter, Tom Loker, and his two partners who he pays fifty dollars to hunt down Eliza and Harry
  • A deal is made that if the capture is Made the hunter's may keep Eliza as a reward
  • Andy and Sam return to the Shelby farm and retell of Eliza's amazing escape
  • Chapter 9
  • Over the River in Ohio, the state senator, Senator Bird, has just assisted in passing a law forbidding the help of runaway slaves, The Fugitive Slave Law of 1850.
  • Bird and his wife who despises slavery, chastises her husband for signing the law, saying she would gladly help a slave if given the opportunity
  • Meanwhile Eliza is brought to the Bird's house where she receives help and direction to a safe house
  • John Van Trompe takes in Eliza and Harry, as he was once a former slave holder who was compelled to change his ways and move north
  • Senator Bird provides Eliza with ten dollars to help with her travels
  • Chapter 10
  • After leaving the tavern Haley returns to the Shelby's farm to take Tom for sale
  • Chloe takes this time to cook her husband a final meal before leaving as she expresses her hatred for slavery while urging Tom to put his faith in God
  • Haley collects Tom and shackles his feet to prepare him to be taken away
  • Mas'r George who was unaware of the sale discovers that Tom is leaving and speaks to him one last time
  • George tells Tom he will purchase his freedom in the future, but for now Tom is given a dollar bill to wear around his neck
  • Chapter 11
  • A small hotel in Kentucky displays a sign for a runaway slave, George, Eliza's husband
  • At the bar men are discussing the sign as it describes the slave as a fair skinned mulatto, who would try to pass himself as a white man, yet to look for a branded H on his hand
  • 400 dollars dead or alive is being offered for the finder of George
  • A man Mr. Wilson, is at the hotel, and recognizes this description as a someone who previously worked for him
  • At this time George enters the inn, appearing to many as a tall Spanish man, and is recognized by Mr. Wilson, yet he refers to himself as Henry Butler and is accompanied by Jim, a slave
  • George looks at the sign and says he seems to have seen a man of that description near a farm earlier in the day in order to throw attention of himself
  • After being recognized as George by Mr. Wilson, George invites him to a room for a discussion
  • Mr. Wilson though good intentioned chastises George for breaking the laws of this country, while George explains his intention to escape to Canada, and asks Mr. Wilson to give Eliza a pin and tell her of George's intention to escape
  • Chapter 12
  • In the meantime, Tom travels with Mr. Haley en route to the slave market.
  • As night approaches they must stop and Tom is forced to stay in jail, thus hurting his pride as he has always been an upstanding man
  • The following day at eleven in the morning the sale begins with Haley purchasing several slaves before the conclusion
  • He puts all the slaves he has purchased on to a ship to be sent to a plantation in the Deep South
  • While aboard and en route south a female slave jumps overboard after being separated from her son as Tom overhears the splash
  • Chapter 13
  • After there escape into Ohio, and the help of others, Eliza and Harry seek refuge at a Quaker Settlement, where they stay with a women by the name of Rachel Halliday
  • Rachel discovers Eliza's last name is Harris, the Quakers put two and two together and realize that a man George Harris, who is supposed to reach the settlement that night, is Eliza's husband
  • The couple is reunited later that night
  • The following day over breakfast, the Quakers and the Harris' discuss a plan for them to escape in the evening
  • Chapter 14
  • The boat carrying Tom and other slaves makes its way down the Mississippi, en route to New Orleans
  • Tom's obedience towards Mr. Haley allows him freedom to walk about the boat as he pleases
  • While having a seat among the cotton bales and reading from his bible Tom meets a young girl by the name of Eva St. Clare
  • She is quite angelic, and spreads happiness among the passengers as she dances about
  • Tom and Eva form a friendship, and she asks her father Augustine to buy Tom rather than let him g to a plantation
  • On the journey, Eva falls overboard, and while everyone looks on in shock Tom jumps in and saves her
  • Augustine is thrilled with Tom, and with a little urge from Eva agrees to buy him from Haley
  • Tom is assigned the job of driving the families coach
  • Chapter 15
  • Chapter 15 discusses the St. Clare family
  • Augustine the father was born in Louisiana, the son of a wealthy planter, and raised by a wonderful mother, who raised him as a soft and gentle person
  • He had fallen in love with a woman from the North yet it was said to be that she married another man, so he ended up settling for his current wife Marie
  • Following his marriage he learned that his true love in fact loved him, and it was a mistake that he learned she married someone else, yet Augustine respected his vows of marriage to Marie
  • Marie, is quite the complainer, she's extremely vain, and a hypochondriac, which ultimately upsets people who know her
  • Augustine, in order to help raise his daughter and take care of his needy wife, brings his cousin, Miss Ophelia, to live with them
  • Though her and Augustine are polar opposites, he is passionate and has different opinions, they love each other very much
  • Following the journey on the boat Augustine, Eva and Tom arrive at the St. Clare's house. Tom is shown around by the black doorman, while Marie and Augustine argue
  • Chapter 16
  • The following day Marie is very annoyed by the slaves, complaining and calling them selfish
  • Eva contradicts her mother saying she could not live without a slave, Mammy, who stays up long nights and comforts Marie
  • Marie retorts by saying Mammy complains too much about being separated from her family
  • When Augustine and Eva finally leave Marie, she continues her rant to Miss Ophelia
  • Eva is very open minded and declares she loves having slaves because they make the house a better place as there are more people to love, and there really is no difference between a black and white person
  • Chapter 17
  • Back with the Quakers, George and Eliza are thrilled to be back together and plan there escape to Canada, knowing it will be long and dangerous
  • Phineas, a Quaker, is to take them to there next check point. He has heard of Loker and his gang being nearby and warns of the danger that awaits
  • The group leaves at dark hoping to avoid there pursuers, they make camp at night in a small area that would make it very difficult for Loker and his men to attack and capture them
  • Loker arrives, yet is met by George who stands up to him, states his intentions to be a free man and says he will defend himself with force
  • Loker and his men shoot at George but do not hit him so Loker tries to get closer, George, true to his word, defends himself and shoots Loker in the side, ultimately forcing the rest of the slave hunters to retreat
  • Eliza feels bad for Loker and insists he is taken to the Quaker settlement to be saved
  • Chapter 18
  • In New Orleans, Tom's role in the family grows, he takes care of the finances for the family, yet his Christian faith leads him to worry for Augustine because he spends many nights drunk and partying
  • While Tom tries to reform Augustine, Miss Ophelia tries to reform the house, cooking, cleaning, and making sure it is done properly
  • A neighbor slave, Prue, comes by the house selling rolls
  • Tom strikes up a conversation with her and learns of her terrible sorrows
  • Prue had a master that would breed slaves, so she lost all her children, when she was sold to a new master she had one more child, however it died of starvation due to lack of help from the masters
  • Tom tells her to put her faith in God
  • Later Eva comes to Tom so he can take her for a ride in her new carriage, however Tom tells the story and Eva is very distraught
  • Chapter 19
  • Later that week Prue is whipped to death by her master and the news travels to the St. Clare's
  • They are all appalled, and Miss Ophelia questions how slavery can be tolerated as well as actions like that
  • It is learned that slaves are property and can be killed by the owner with no repercussions
  • Augustine expresses his disgust but shows that he is fine by letting slavery occur because he cant change it
  • Augustine also tells of how he struggled being a master of many slaves because he felt remorse for him
  • He tells a story of one who tried to escape and was caught, Augustine offers to free him, but the slave is grateful of Augustine's offer and pledges his life to him
  • Eva is brought to tears from these stories, and later Tom writes a letter to his family, and Eva helps him
  • Chapter 20
  • Augustine buys a new slave, Topsy, a young female
  • She's never been educated so it is up to Miss Ophelia to tutor her
  • Ophelia is reluctant but is told to look at it as a missionary role
  • Ophelia struggles with Topsy as she is very wild and doesn't like to listen, Topsy steals, and Ophelia doesn't know how to work with her as when she tries to teach her the Catechism, she doesn't retain anything
  • The only sign of Topsy's progression is when Eva speaks kindly to her as Topsy has never been spoken to that way
  • Chapter 21
  • In Kentucky at the Shelby farm Aunt Chloe receives Tom's letter and is relieved to hear how well he is doing
  • The Shelby's are still in debt yet Mrs. Shelby wants to buy Tom back
  • Chloe, tells her there is no need as he is doing fine, and asks Mrs. Shelby if she could work selling pies on her own time in order to make money for Tom, and she obliges
  • Chapter 22
  • Two years go by while Tom is living with the St. Clare's
  • Tom and Eva develop a fond relationship, and share a passion for Christianity
  • Mas'r George updates Tom on his life and education in Kentucky
  • The St. Clare takes a vacation to Lake Ponchartrain where Tom and Eva spend many hours reading from the bible and singing hymns
  • They talk of angels and Eva mentions that she will be with them very soon which worries Tom
  • Miss Ophelia calls Eva inside as she is worried about her being ill, Eva has developed a cough, and Tom has recognized she is getting very thin
  • Chapter 23
  • Augustine's brother comes for a visit bringing his son Henrique to socialize with Eva
  • Henrique is seen striking his slave by Eva and she is very upset, she apologizes to the slave, Dodo, for his actions and asks Henrique to love his slave, to which he agrees
  • Augustine and his brother witness this and argue about slavery, Augustine believes the slaves will rise up, while his brother states he ill oppress them as long as possible
  • Chapter 24
  • Eva's health continues to deteriorate, and for the first time Marie shows compassion for her child, however she acts like she is affected the most by her daughters health
  • It appears as if Eva might recover but that is short lived, however her poor health doesn't stop her compassion or love for the slaves, she even tells Tom she would die for the slaves if it saved them pain
  • Eva tells her father that she feels as if she will soon die, but pleads to him to free all his slaves after she dies, he only agrees to free Tom, but does says he will try to help out the slaves
  • Chapter 25
  • One afternoon while Marie and Augustine are conversing, Ophelia interrupts saying she can no longer take care of Topsy
  • Eva thinking she can help and takes Topsy aside, she asks if she loves anyone, and stresses that Ophelia will love her if she is good
  • She also states that she loves her and so does Jesus, and that for there sake can she be good to Ophelia
  • The adults overhear this, and Ophelia admits she has a prejudice towards blacks, she vows to try to shed some of the prejudice
  • Chapter 26
  • Eva asks to have her hair cut and to speak to all the slaves, she tells them she loves them and to be good Christians and love one another, and lastly gives them all a lock of her hair to remember her
  • She then speaks to her father and asks him of his beliefs and tells him of heaven, however he does not seem to see her beliefs
  • Eva dies soon after as her mother, father, and Ophelia watch; her last words are "Oh! Love, -joy, -peace"
  • Chapter 27
  • Following the funeral the whole house is in morning, yet Marie screams at them to do her bidding
  • Augustine is very distraught yet can't shed a tear, Tom tries to teach him about God yet can not get through
  • It is not until Augustine hears Tom praying for him that he finally feels an inkling of faith
  • Chapter 28
  • Ophelia finally gets through and reforms Topsy, she asks to become owner of her so she can set her free to live a Christian life, Augustine agrees with her
  • She then asks him if he has arranged for the other slaves to be taken care of after his death, and he says he will provide for them, so as to not have them sent to cruel owners
  • Later that day, while at a cafe, St. Clare is subject to the presence of a fight and is stabbed and is slowly dying
  • He is brought home where he speaks to Tom, and asks him to pray for him, as he is finally going home, just before death he speaks out the word "Mother!"
  • Chapter 29
  • Augustine's sudden death did not allow him time to make arrangements for his slaves even though he had promised Eva
  • Marie now owns the slaves, she first has one whipped, then puts the rest up to sale at a slave warehouse despite Ophelia's objections
  • Chapter 30
  • Two new slaves are introduced, Susan, and her beautiful daughter Emmeline, at the slave warehouse
  • Susan is fearful of her daughter being sold for sex so she tries to make her as unattractive as possible, however the seller has her made up so as to make more money for the sale
  • A cruel man Simon Legree, purchases both Tom and Emmeline, as well as two other men
  • Chapter 31
  • Legree chains up Tom, takes everything he owns, and puts him on the slave ship to go to his plantation
  • He tells the slaves he doesn't allow religion and threatens them with his fist
  • Chapter 32
  • On the voyage Legree makes snide remarks at Emmeline, hinting that he will use her for sex
  • Legree has two slaves be overseers for him on the ship and creates a gap between them and the others, he also has a slave women living in his quarters, Cassy, who he plans to replace with Emmeline
  • The new plantation is horrific, and Tom's religion are tested
  • Chapter 33
  • Tom is working in the fields when he sees two women struggling, Cassy and another slave women, to do there work
  • He helps them, yet it is reported by the overseers to Legree who orders Tom to whip the two women
  • He refuses and in turn is beat nearly to death
  • Chapter 34
  • Cassy tends to Tom after his beating and tries to heal him
  • She laments to him that there is no hope, no God, but Tom stops her, telling her not to let terrible acts by terrible people to hurt her faith
  • Cassy is a mulatto, previously she was born to a rich white man, and mothered children with a lawyer, however he grew tired of her and sold her and there children
  • She then had her children sold away and she was sold to a different master, she had a child from a previous master yet killed it in order to not lose it, and eventually after being passed from master to master she wound up with Legree
  • Chapter 35
  • Legree grew attached to Cassy, all the while she scorned him and the way he treated her and his slaves
  • One day while Cassy and Legree are arguing, Sambo, a overseer, brings to Legree a lock of Eva's hair that Tom had around his neck, Legree twinges the moment he touches it, and screams as if it came alive, he then throws it in the fire
  • Legree's past is disclosed in this chapter, he was very close to his mother who loved him and treated him well while his father was very harsh
  • Legree went to sea, and while there he received a letter from his mother with a lock of her hair, as she was dying
  • Touching Eva's hair brings back sad memories of losing his mother
  • He turns to drinking following this, and in turn leaves his house in search of Emmeline, he however hears slaves singing hymns and he feels Tom's spiritual power which he fears greatly
  • Chapter 36
  • Cassy has begged Legree to leave Tom alone, however the following day he ignores her and forces Tom to his knees and demands him to beg for a pardon
  • Tom refuses as he says Legree can do as he wills, but in the end nothing harms him
  • Chapter 37
  • Eliza and George travel on to another Quaker settlement, they leave Loker behind at the previous one to be healed
  • Loker, thankful for being saved, changes his sinful ways and lives with the Quakers
  • George and Eliza disguise themselves and finally reach Canada, and a free life
  • Chapter 38
  • At the plantation Tom feels his faith begin to falter once again, yet as he sings a hymn he sees Jesus and his faith is renewed, keeping him strong even when being beaten
  • Cassy one night tells Tom she wants to murder Legree
  • Tom demands that it is a sin and she shouldn't, instead she should escape
  • Chapter 39
  • Cassy makes a daring plan that involves her convincing Legree there are ghosts in the attic
  • Then her and Emmeline, make an escape to a nearby swamp, as a search party goes after them, they sneak back into the attic, where they plan to stay for a while, all the while any noises they make will be attributed to ghosts
  • Chapter 40
  • Legree takes his rage of the escape out on Tom beating him all night when Tom refuses to give him any information
  • He later passes the beating on to two other slaves, Tom continues to remain faithful, and even pushes Christianity on the two slaves beating them, hoping they will be saved
  • Chapter 41
  • A few days later, Mas'r George, Mr. Shelby's son, shows up at Legree's plantation, where he plans to buy Tom back
  • George finds Tom near death and they share a moment before Tom slips away at peace
  • George enraged at the beating threatens to have Legree charged, and eventually attacks Legree
  • As he is leaving with Tom's body other slaves ask if he will free him, he however can't afford them but promises to fight to abolish slavery
  • Chapter 42
  • Cassy and Emmeline finally make there escape, and end up on the boat with Mas'r George
  • Cassy tells her story and he promises to help them
  • Later on the boat George speaks with a French woman, who after asking questions realizes the other George, George Harris is her brother
  • Cassy overhears the conversation and realizes that Eliza might be her daughter after she is described
  • Chapter 43
  • Cassy, Emmeline, and the French woman, Madame de Thoux, travel to Montreal, where they meet George and Eliza
  • George has a new job, and Eliza has had another child
  • The whole family is finally reunited, and decide to travel to France where they live for a few years
  • They return to the United States, but eventually after learning of a West African nation, Liberia, that was established for freed slaves
  • Chapter 44/li>
  • Mas'r George returns to the farm where he informs Chloe of Tom's death, he then frees all his slaves
  • They, afraid to leave, are willing to still work for George, and he agrees to pay them
  • He tells them about Tom's death and to think of there freedom each time they look at his cabin
  • Chapter 45
  • This chapter is primarily an epilogue as the author urges the Northerners and Southerners to abolish slavery in the name of God
